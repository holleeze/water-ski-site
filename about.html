OUR STORY

The Saskatoon Water Ski Club (SWSC) is a non-profit organization operated by its members on a volunteer basis. The club has served Saskatoon watersports enthusiasts since 1972 and is the oldest water ski club in Canada.

SWSC operates exclusively at the “Rathole,” a storm pond created during the construction of the 51st Street overpass. The site features a clay bottom and measures approximately 825 metres long, 80 metres wide, and up to 4 metres deep. Its gently sloped banks and gradual waterline help minimize backwash and allow efficient use of the full course length and width.

The facility includes a clubhouse, tournament centre, and fully accessible portable restroom facilities during the regular operating season. On the water, SWSC offers a record-capable six-ball slalom course, an adaptive slalom course, a regulation jump, and a jump that can be lowered to three feet for beginners and skiers with disabilities.

The Rathole is located in the City of Saskatoon, southwest of Faithful Avenue and 58th Street. Access is easiest from 58th Street: take 51st Street to Faithful Avenue, travel north on Faithful, then go one block west on 58th Street and look south.
